My Journey

From a small village in Bihar to building things that matter.

2003 · Origin
Pai Bigha, Gaya, Bihar

Born in a small village in Gaya district, Bihar. The kind of place that teaches you hunger before it teaches you anything else. Every aspiration felt big because the starting point was small.

2020–2022 · Foundation
JEE Preparation

Two years of relentless preparation. Physics, Mathematics, Chemistry. Learned that consistency beats talent when talent doesn't show up. Started seeing systems everywhere — in equations, in problems, in life.

2022 · Arrival
Lovely Professional University

Landed at LPU, one of India's largest universities. Overwhelmed at first — 25,000 students, hundreds of clubs, infinite possibilities. Chose to build rather than just attend.

2023 · First Build
Discovering AI & ML

Fell deep into artificial intelligence and machine learning. Not as a trend, but as a tool. Started seeing how algorithms could solve problems that human systems had been ignoring for decades.

2024 · First Launch
Study LPU

During midterm season, noticed a painful gap: LPU students had no structured place to practice MCQs in a real exam environment. Built Study LPU in weeks. Today: 300+ active students, 1,835+ questions solved.

2025 · Community
Founders Verbinden

Founded Founders Verbinden to solve a different problem: founders building in isolation. Created a platform connecting builders, mentors, and investors — because the right connection at the right time changes everything.

2026 · Tradition
MangalKit

My mother held our first prototype like a blessing. She said: "Beta, yeh wahi lag raha hai jo mandir se milta tha." That moment became the mission — devotion, wrapped with care. MangalKit was born.

Mar 2026 · Published
The Rhythm Code

Published first book — "The Rhythm Code: Ancient Wisdom for Modern Burnout" on Amazon Kindle. A science-backed guide blending Ayurvedic wisdom with modern neuroscience. Co-authored with Shivam Kumar Singh & Vinita Kashyap.

Now · Horizon
What Comes Next

Research. Writing. Open source. Growing each venture into something sustainable. Building at the intersection of AI, community, and purpose — from Pai Bigha to the world.

Builder Philosophy

What I believe
about building.

01 Technology should solve real problems.

Not imagined ones. The best tech is invisible — it removes friction so quietly that people forget it wasn't always there.

02 Communities should create opportunities.

Networks that only look good on paper are useless. A community earns its name when a member's life actually changes because of it.

03 Tradition should evolve, not disappear.

MangalKit is proof. Devotion is timeless. The way we deliver it shouldn't be stuck in 1990. Respect the root, modernise the path.

04 Learning should never stop.

From JEE prep to AI research — the discipline is the same. Understand first. Build second. Ship third. Repeat.

05 Where you start doesn't determine where you go.

Pai Bigha, Bihar. Population: small. Ambition: unlimited. The gap between the two is just work and time.
