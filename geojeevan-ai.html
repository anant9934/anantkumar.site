01 · Problem

Health data was everywhere. Meaningful health intelligence was nowhere.

India has AQI dashboards, dengue alerts, heat warnings — all scattered across separate government portals that nobody opens. Even if you find the data, it tells you nothing actionable. A number like "AQI: 187" means nothing to most people without context, comparison, and a clear "so what?"

Meanwhile, the actual health consequences of living in polluted, high-heat, mosquito-prone environments are profoundly underappreciated until it's too late. By the time someone ends up in a hospital for a heat stroke or dengue fever, ten early warning signs were missed.

The question GeoJeevan was built to answer: What is my actual health risk, right now, based on where I am and what's in my environment?

02 · Solution

One score. Real data. Early warning.

GeoJeevan AI pulls together environmental data — air quality, temperature, humidity, disease prevalence by region — and uses AI to synthesise a single, meaningful health risk score. Not a raw number. A verdict with context: what's driving the risk, which factors are most dangerous right now, and what you can do about it.

The interface is deliberately simple. You shouldn't need to be an environmental scientist to understand whether it's safe to go for a morning run or whether your child should be kept indoors today. The science is hidden behind the product. The answer is on the surface.

We're building toward daily use — because health risk changes every day. The goal is for GeoJeevan to become the kind of tool you check as instinctively as a weather app.

03 · Technology

AI-powered. Environment-aware. Real-time.

GeoJeevan integrates multiple environmental data streams and fuses them through an AI model trained on health outcome correlations. The system accounts for: air quality index (AQI) and its breakdowns by pollutant, ambient temperature and heat index, regional dengue and disease outbreak data, and seasonal pattern modelling.

The platform is live and actively seeking user feedback to improve the model, add new health risk categories, and expand geographic coverage across Indian cities.

04 · The Ask

Would you trust this enough to use it daily?

This is the question GeoJeevan launched with on LinkedIn, and it's the right question. Trust is the entire product when it comes to health information. We built something real — not a prototype, not a demo. But real trust takes time and iteration.

User feedback drives what GeoJeevan builds next. If you have an opinion on what's missing — a risk category that matters to you, a region that should be supported, a feature that would change how you use it — that feedback matters here more than anywhere else.
